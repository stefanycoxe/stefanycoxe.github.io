Research

My research focuses on evaluating and applying advanced statistical methods to behavioral data. My research falls into two broad categories: quantitative research and applied research.

CV / Google Scholar

Quantitative research

Generalized linear model (GLiM)

GLiMs are a broad family of statistical models that extend linear regression analysis to a wider range of outcome variable distributions and error structures. I use simulations to explore the performance of these models. I am particularly interested in the statistical characteristics of counts outcome variables, especially in statistical mediation models.

Integrative data analysis (IDA)

I am joint co-PI on TIDAL, an integrative data analysis project funded by NIMH combining data from 4 separate psychosocial interventions for teens who have been diagnosed with ADHD. The combined dataset is publicly available here. Among the methodological challenges in this project were harmonization of measures of ADHD symptoms (DSM-IV versus DSM-5) and parent depression symptoms (various measures) using moderated nonlinear factor analysis.

Statistical graphics and R shiny

I am interested in graphics as an aid to interpreting and communicating statistical models. To this end, I have developed shiny applications to calculate effect size for count regression models and mediation models. shiny applications are interactive, web-based tools for statistical analysis and graphics.

  • RcountD: Estimate effect sizes for regression models for count models (Poisson regression and negative binomial regression). Standard multiplicative effect size (rate ratio) and standardized mean difference (Cohen’s d) effect sizes are provided. Monte Carlo confidence intervals for each effect size are also given.
    • Coxe, S. (2018, February). Effect size measures for nonlinear count regression models. Poster presented at the American Statistical Association Conference on Statistical Practice. Portland, OR. February 15 – 17, 2018.
  • SimpleMediation: Estimate indirect (mediated) effect with Monte Carlo simulated confidence intervals. This app was developed for pedagogical purposes.

Applied research

My applied research takes place largely in the context of clinical and psychosocial interventions. I am affiliated with the Center for Children and Families (CCF) at Florida International University, where I help evaluate aspects of the Summer Treatment Program (STP) for children and adolescents with attention-deficit-hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), interventions to improve infant and toddler mental health, and child mental health adjustment following traumatic events. These studies include complex longitudinal designs, non-normally distributed outcome variables, and extensive missing data. Previously, I worked at the Prevention Research Center (PRC) at Arizona State University on longitudinal and prospective evaluations of interventions for bereaved children and Mexican-American adolescents, including psychometric analysis of study measures, longitudinal growth models, multilevel regression, and multilevel structural equation models.

Advanced quantitative training

All students completing a Ph.D. in the Psychology department (Ph.D. in Psychology or Ph.D. in Cognitive Neuroscience) are required to complete three common courses covering introductory topics in applied statistics and quantitative methods: Quantitative Methods I (PSY 5939), Quantitative Methods II (PSY 5939), and Multivariate Analysis in Applied Psychological Research (PSY 5246C).

Students who complete three (3) additional advanced quantitative methods electives, for a total of 6 statistics courses, can receive a departmental acknowledgement that they have completed advanced quantitative training. Students must achieve a minimum of B- in each course. Electives may be selected from the list of approved courses below, or contact Dr. Stefany Coxe (stefany.coxe@fiu.edu) for approval of a different course.

Should you have any questions about the content and rigor of our advanced quantitative courses, please feel free to contact Dr. Shannon Pruden at (sdick@fiu.edu) or (305) 348-2784.
